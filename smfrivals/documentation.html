Overview

SMF Rivals is a comprehensive competitive gaming modification for Simple Machines Forum (SMF) 2.1. It transforms your forum into a full-featured competitive gaming hub with clan management, ranked ladders, tournaments, leagues, and more.

Key Features

  • Clan System — Create and manage gaming teams with members, rosters, and internal chat
  • Ranked Ladders — Compete on ladders with ELO, Swap, or RTH ranking systems
  • Challenge & Match System — Challenge opponents, report results, track detailed statistics
  • Tournaments — Single elimination, double elimination, round robin, and league formats
  • Seasons & Leagues — Organize competitions into seasons with archived standings
  • MVP Awards — Recognize top performers in matches and tournaments
  • Decerto & CPC Modes — Multi-round match formats with random map/mode selection
  • Football (Calcio) Mode — Goal-based ladder variant for sports games
  • Profile Integration — Gamer tags, clan info, and stats on user profiles
  • Alerts — Real-time notifications for challenges, match results, clan invites, and more

Installation

Log in to your SMF forum as an administrator.

Go to Admin > Packages.

Upload the smfrivals.tar.gz or smfrivals.zip package.

Click Install and follow the prompts. The installer will create all database tables, register hooks, and add the Rivals menu to your forum.

Go to Admin > Rivals to configure your settings.

Note: After installation, you must enable the mod by setting Rivals Enabled to Yes in the admin settings before it becomes visible to users.

Your Profile

Once Rivals is installed, a new Rivals section appears in every user's profile page. Here you can see and manage your competitive identity.

Gamer Name

Your gamer name is your in-game identity displayed throughout the Rivals system. You can set or change it on your own profile. Administrators can also edit any user's gamer name.

Profile Stats

Your profile displays the following information:

  • Clan — Your current clan and role (Leader, Co-Leader, or Member)
  • Ladder Standings — Every ladder you're enrolled in, with your rank, score, and W-L-D record
  • Tournament History — Your last 10 tournament participations
  • Experience & Level — Your accumulated XP and current level
  • MVP Count — Total MVP awards received

Clans

Clans are the heart of team-based competition in Rivals. They represent your gaming team and allow you to challenge other clans on ladders and enter tournaments together.

Browsing Clans

The Clans page shows all active clans in a sortable directory. You can filter clans by:

  • Alphabetical — Click any letter A-Z to filter by name
  • Ladder — Filter to show only clans in a specific ladder

Creating a Clan

Go to Rivals > Clans and click Create Clan.

Enter your clan name (must be unique, at least 2 characters).

Add an optional description, website URL, and logo image.

Click Create. You automatically become the clan Leader.

Note: The administrator may require a minimum number of forum posts before you can create a clan. You also cannot lead more than one clan at the same time.

Joining a Clan

To join an existing clan, visit the clan's profile page and click Join Clan. Your request will be marked as Pending until a clan leader approves it.

Joining a Ladder

To compete on a ladder, visit the ladder's standings page and click Join Ladder. You (or your clan) will be added to the bottom of the standings with a starting score (1500 for ELO, 50 for RTH).

Reading the Standings

The standings table shows each competitor's current position. Columns include:

  • Rank — Current position (with arrow showing movement since last update)
  • Name — Clan or player name
  • Score — Current rating (ELO points, swap rank, or RTH points)
  • W-L-D — Wins, Losses, and Draws
  • Streak — Recent results (e.g. WWLWW)
  • Status — Activity icons (see Status Icons)

If Advanced Stats are enabled for the ladder, you'll also see per-player statistics: kills, deaths, assists, K/D ratio, goals, and MVP count.

Freezing Your Position

If you need a break from competition, you can Freeze your position on a ladder. While frozen, your rank is preserved but you cannot challenge or be challenged. Unfreezing restores you to active competition.

Freeze Cost: Freezing may incur a score penalty (configured by the administrator, default 25%). This prevents abuse of the freeze system.

Challenges & Matches

The challenge and match system is how competition happens on ladders. Here's the complete workflow from challenge to confirmed result.

Match Workflow

Create Challenge
→
Accept/Decline
→
Play Match
→
Report Result
→
Confirm/Dispute
→
Rankings Updated

Step 1: Creating a Challenge

Navigate to the ladder standings where you want to compete.

Click on an opponent's name and select Challenge, or go to Rivals > Challenge.

Select the ladder, choose your opponent, and optionally add match details.

Choose whether the match is Ranked (affects standings) or Unranked (practice only).

Submit the challenge. Your opponent receives an alert notification.

Challenge Rules:
  • Both parties must be enrolled in the same ladder
  • You cannot challenge yourself or your own clan
  • Only one open challenge per opponent per ladder at a time
  • In Swap ranking mode, your opponent must be within 3 ranks of you

Step 2: Accepting or Declining

The challenged party views their incoming challenges and can:

  • Accept — A match record is created. For Decerto/CPC ladders, random maps and modes are generated at this point.
  • Decline — The challenge is cancelled. In RTH mode, declining 3 or more times earns a "Chicken" penalty (see RTH Ranking).

Step 3: Reporting the Result

After playing your match, the winner goes to Rivals > My Match and reports the result:

  • Enter the final score for each side
  • For Decerto/CPC matches, enter per-round scores with maps and modes
  • Optionally select up to 3 MVP players (if enabled on the ladder)
  • Add any match notes or comments
Time Limit: Match results must be reported within the reporting window (default: 72 hours). After this period, the match expires and cannot be reported.

Step 4: Confirmation

The other party receives an alert to confirm the reported result. They can:

  • Confirm — The match is finalized. Rankings are updated based on the result and the ladder's ranking system.
  • Dispute — The match is flagged for moderator review. An administrator or the ladder's assigned moderator will review the evidence and decide the outcome.

Match Chat

Each match has a comments section where both parties can discuss the match, coordinate schedules, or resolve minor disagreements before the result is finalized.

Match History

All completed matches are visible on the Matches page, showing recent results across all ladders with scores, participants, and completion dates.

Go to Rivals > Tournaments and find a tournament with Signups Open status.

Click Sign Up. If the tournament requires a roster, select which team members will participate.

If the tournament is invite-only, you can only sign up if your clan or user ID is on the invite list.

Once the tournament is active, you can view the bracket at Rivals > Brackets. The bracket shows:

  • All matchups organized by round (Round 1, Quarter-Finals, Semi-Finals, Final, etc.)
  • Completed matches with scores highlighted
  • Winners advancing to the next round
  • BYE rounds if the number of entrants isn't a perfect power of 2
  • For double elimination: both the Winners Bracket and Losers Bracket

Tournament Options

  • Clan or Individual — Tournaments can be team-based (clans enter) or user-based (individuals enter)
  • Bracket Size — 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, or 128 slots
  • Roster Restrictions — Minimum and maximum team size requirements
  • Advanced Stats — Track per-player kills/deaths/assists throughout the tournament
  • Decerto Mode — Use the multi-round Decerto format for tournament matches

Leagues & Seasons

Seasons

Ladders can be organized into Seasons. A season represents a competitive period (e.g. "Spring 2026" or "Season 3"). When a season ends:

  • All current standings are archived for historical viewing
  • Standings are reset (scores return to 1500, ranks are reassigned)
  • A new season can begin with everyone starting fresh

You can view past seasons on the Season History page, which shows final standings for each completed season.

League Standings

League-type tournaments use a football-style points system:

ResultPoints
Win3 points
Draw1 point
Loss0 points

Tiebreakers are resolved in this order: total points, then goal difference (GF - GA), then goals scored.

Match Finder

The Match Finder is a matchmaking queue that helps you find opponents quickly.

Go to Rivals > Match Finder.

Select a ladder and choose whether you want a ranked or unranked match.

Click Join Queue. You'll be added to the waiting list.

When a suitable opponent joins the same queue, a challenge is automatically created for both of you.

You can leave the queue at any time before a match is found.

MVP Awards

MVP (Most Valuable Player) awards recognize outstanding individual performances in matches.

  • After a match is reported, up to 3 MVP players can be selected from the participants
  • MVP awards are only available on ladders and tournaments where the administrator has enabled them
  • Your total MVP count is displayed on your profile and clan membership

MVP Leaderboard

The MVP page shows the top players by total MVP count across all ladders. The MVP Chart breaks this down per ladder.

Leaderboard

The global Leaderboard page shows the top 100 players across the entire Rivals system, ranked by their combined experience, level, and performance stats.

ELO Ranking System

The ELO system is the default ranking method, based on the same algorithm used in chess. It provides a fair skill-based rating where your score changes based on both the result and the relative strength of your opponent.

How It Works

  • Starting Score: 1500 points
  • Beating a higher-rated opponent gains more points than beating a lower-rated one
  • Losing to a lower-rated opponent costs more points than losing to a higher-rated one
  • Draws give a small adjustment toward the expected outcome

K-Factor

The K-factor determines how much a single match can change your score. It decreases as your rating increases, providing stability for established players:

Rating RangeK-FactorEffect
Below 200030Large swings — developing players move quickly to their true rating
2000 – 240020Moderate changes — intermediate stability
Above 240010Small adjustments — established players maintain consistent ratings

Swap Ranking System

The Swap system is a simple positional ranking. Instead of numerical scores, competitors hold rank positions (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.).

How It Works

  • When you win against a higher-ranked opponent, you take their rank and they drop one position.
  • If you beat a lower-ranked opponent, nothing changes — you keep your own rank.
  • You can only challenge opponents within 3 ranks of your current position.
  • The system tracks your best rank (highest achieved) and worst rank (lowest dropped to).
Tip: The Swap system is ideal for smaller, tight-knit ladders where a clear "king of the hill" ranking is preferred over numerical scores.

RTH (Race to the Top) Ranking

The RTH system is a points accumulation race. The goal is to be the first competitor to reach the target score.

How It Works

  • Starting Score: 50 points
  • Win matches to gain points toward the goal
  • First to reach the top score wins the ladder

Chicken Penalty

In RTH mode, declining challenges has consequences:

  • After 3 or more declined challenges, you receive the "Chicken" status
  • A chicken icon appears next to your name in the standings
  • This mechanic encourages active competition and discourages avoiding tough opponents

Decerto & CPC Match Formats

Decerto and CPC are multi-round match formats designed for competitive FPS (First-Person Shooter) games. They add variety and fairness by randomizing the conditions for each round.

Decerto Mode

A Decerto match consists of 3 rounds, each with a randomly selected game mode AND a randomly selected map:

Example Decerto Match (Call of Duty)

RoundGame ModeMapScore
1Search & DestroyCrash6 - 4
2DominationBacklot185 - 200
3Capture the FlagStrike3 - 1

The winner is determined by the aggregate score across all 3 rounds.

CPC Mode

CPC is similar to Decerto but simpler. It consists of 3 rounds with random maps only — the game mode stays the same for all rounds.

Example CPC Match

RoundGame ModeMapScore
1Team DeathmatchOvergrown75 - 60
2Team DeathmatchVacant62 - 75
3Team DeathmatchPipeline75 - 68

How Random Selection Works

When a challenge is accepted on a Decerto or CPC ladder, the system automatically:

  1. Selects game modes and maps from the pool configured by the administrator
  2. Ensures no duplicate modes (Decerto) or maps (both) are picked
  3. Displays the selections to both parties so they know what to play
Admin Setup Required: Decerto and CPC modes require the administrator to configure game modes and maps in Admin > Rivals > Game Modes. Each mode needs at least 3 maps configured before it can be used.

Football (Calcio) Mode

The Football ladder style is designed for sports games (FIFA, PES, etc.) where goals are the primary scoring metric.

Key Differences from Standard

  • Goals For / Goals Against are tracked instead of generic scores
  • Goal Difference (GF - GA) is used as a tiebreaker
  • Draws are common and expected (unlike FPS ladders where ties are rare)

Tournament Workflow

Create the tournament with status Draft. Configure all settings.

Change status to Signups Open. Teams/players can now register.

When enough entries are received, change status to Active. The bracket is generated automatically.

Participants play and report matches. The bracket advances automatically.

After the final match, change status to Archived for permanent record-keeping.

Admin: Managing Seasons

Seasons allow you to organize ladder competition into time-bounded periods.

Season Lifecycle

Start Season — Select a ladder and name the season (e.g. "Season 5 - Summer 2026"). The season becomes active.

Competition runs normally on the ladder. All results count toward the active season.

End Season — All current standings are archived. Scores reset to 1500. Ranks are reassigned sequentially. The season is marked as completed.

Important: Only one season can be active per ladder at a time. You must end the current season before starting a new one.

Archived seasons are viewable by all users on the Season History page.

Admin: Game Modes & Maps

This section manages the game modes and maps used by Decerto and CPC ladder styles.

Structure

Game modes are organized in a parent-child hierarchy:

  • Game Modes (parent) — e.g. "Search & Destroy", "Domination", "Capture the Flag"
  • Maps (child) — e.g. "Crash", "Backlot", "Strike" — each linked to a parent mode

Adding a Game Mode

  • Name — Full mode name
  • Short Name — Abbreviated identifier (used to link modes to ladders)
  • Type — "Game Mode" or "Map"
  • Parent Mode — For maps, select which game mode they belong to
  • CPC Flag — Mark as CPC-only mode
  • Active — Toggle availability. Modes need at least 3 maps before they can be used.
Tip: For Decerto to work properly, configure exactly 3 game modes per game, each with at least 3 maps. The system will randomly select one mode and one map per round.

Admin: Random Map of the Day

The Random Map feature displays a randomly selected map each day on a dedicated page. It adds variety and discussion to the community.

Managing the Map Pool

  • Add maps with a name, short name, and optional image
  • The system randomly selects one map each day for display
  • Add as many maps as you like — more maps mean more variety

Admin: Match Moderation

The Matches section provides tools for moderating and resolving match disputes.

Features

  • Filter by status — View pending, completed, or disputed matches
  • View details — See full match data including scores, participants, maps, and timestamps
  • Resolve disputes — Choose the winner for disputed matches (Challenger wins, Challengee wins, or Draw)
  • Delete matches — Remove invalid or test matches from the system

Dispute Resolution Workflow

A match is disputed by one of the participants.

The match appears in the admin panel with Disputed status.

Review match chat, submitted scores, and any evidence provided by both parties.

Select a resolution: Challenger Wins, Challengee Wins, or Draw.

Rankings are automatically updated based on your decision.

Ladder Moderators: If a ladder has an assigned moderator (set in ladder configuration), that user can also resolve disputes for that specific ladder without needing full admin access.
